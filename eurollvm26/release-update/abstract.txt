LLVM’s release process is an ever-evolving chaos machine. Since the last presentation on the process in 2023, we’ve made changes and continued to refine how we ship. Over the past three years, we’ve delivered six releases and learned quite a bit along the way. In this talk, we’ll cover what changed, why it changed, and introduce our two new Release Managers.
We’ll also discuss what works well today, and what tends to create stress and frustration for both release managers and developers.
Making an LLVM release is a team sport, and it’s important that we have understanding and buy-in across the LLVM community. If you contribute to LLVM, depend on LLVM, or simply want fewer surprises when “the release is coming,” this is meant to be a candid, practical tour of where we are today and where we should go next.
